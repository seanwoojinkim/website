about

designer, developer, and artist exploring the intersection of computation and human experience.

background

i'm sean kim, a designer and developer based in brooklyn, new york. i create interactive systems that explore human connection through computational media—spanning generative art, biometric installations, and experimental interfaces that make invisible patterns of behavior visible and tangible.

founder of wooj (named after my middle name, woojin), a lighting company I started in spring 2021. we design and 3d-print cordless lamps from recycled plastic in our brooklyn studio—26 printers running made-to-order production. everything designed, manufactured, assembled, and shipped in-house. the viral wavy lamp series democratizes boutique design through accessible manufacturing.

currently developing biometric coherence installations that visualize physiological synchrony between people through real-time generative systems. exploring how technology can foster contemplative connection rather than distraction.

practice

my work operates at the intersection of physical product design, computational systems, and experiential art. i'm interested in how objects and interfaces can create moments of contemplation, connection, and presence.

areas of focus:

  • generative systems and emergent behavior
  • biometric sensing and physiological computing
  • product design and manufacturing
  • interactive art installations
  • real-time rendering and animation
  • contemplative technology
  • hardware prototyping and embedded systems

approach

i work across disciplines—from low-level sensor integration (ecg, ppg, biometric data) to high-level visualization systems (p5.js, canvas, webgl), to physical product development and manufacturing processes.

technical: modular architecture, es6 modules, component-based design, hardware prototyping (esp32, arduino), signal processing.

aesthetic: swiss minimalism, precise typography, functional beauty, honest materials.

collaborations

i'm interested in working with people and organizations on projects that push the boundaries of what's possible at the intersection of technology, design, and human experience.

particularly interested in:

  • interactive art installations and public art commissions
  • biometric / physiological sensing projects
  • product design and manufacturing innovation
  • generative and computational design systems
  • experimental interfaces and tools
  • research at the intersection of art and science
